Popular destinations in Egypt

Major tourist destinations include the millennia-old monuments in the Nile Valley, with the Pyramids and Great Sphinx at Giza being the most iconic.

Luxor, located about 500 km south of Cairo, is the site of the ancient city of Thebes. It includes the ruins of the temple complexes at Karnak and Luxor, which stand within the modern city. On the opposite side of the Nile River lie the monuments, temples, and tombs of the West Bank Necropolis, home to the Valley of the Kings and the Valley of the Queens.

Sinai is home to the beach resorts of Sharm el-Sheikh, Dahab, Nuweiba, and Taba, along with biblical sites like Mount Sinai (“Jabal Musa”). The nearby Saint Catherine’s Monastery, believed to be the oldest working Christian monastery in the world, adds to the region’s historical significance.
